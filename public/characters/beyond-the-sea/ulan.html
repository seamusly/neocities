Summary
  • Ulán grew up with his older sister, his mother, and his abusive uncle; the latter of which was a drunkard with a gambling addiction.
  • As he was growing, he tried to fight his uncle to throw him out of the house, only to be beaten himself. He similarly was facing abuse from bullies at school.
  • One fateful day while he was being confronted by his bullies, a boy named Masaru stood up to them and spurred Ulán on to fight back. Together, they beat the bullies down and became close friends.
  • Over the years, Ulán's hatred towards his uncle grew and he plotted with Masaru to "trap" the man before handing him over to the local mob -- to which Ulán's uncle owed money.
  • Ulán stole money from his uncle that was meant to appease the loan sharks on the day that it was due. When his uncle tried to fight him, he and Masaru beat him bloody before leaving him to his fate.
  • Following that, the pair left Onekama for a few years to travel.
  • Eventually, Ulán wished to try adventuring as a career and so they traveled to Ethovalon to sign up to the GCSF. In Ethovalon, Ulán and Masaru wound up arrested for property damage.
  • Opting to work out their sentence with the guild, they were assigned to the team Four Seasons and told to keep their antics to a minimum.
Backstory

As a child, Ulán's home life was tumultuous. He lived with his older sister, his mother, and his uncle who had been residing at their home for a time after losing his job and being taken in by Ulán's father. When he was roughly 5-years old, Ulán's father had vanished, and so Ulán's uncle had taken over as the head of the family.

Though things had initially started off well, Ulán's uncle eventually proved himself to be an abusive individual. He was a gambling addict who was frequently drunk, constantly mishandling the house's finances and getting involved with shady individuals to borrow money as a result. While Ulán was growing, his mother and sister would often try to protect him from the truth of their situation by downplaying the damage that was being done to them. However, as he grew into a young teen, it became more and more difficult to for them to do so.

On several occasions, Ulán attempted to physically fight his uncle, only to be beaten down himself as he was still a mere child. His home life began to affect him psychologically, and he grew embittered. At school, he was bullied for being asocial and -- perhaps out of habit -- he had a tendency to grit his teeth and bear it.

On one fateful day however, Ulán was shoved into a younger boy who decided to stand up for him. The boy was even smaller than Ulán was at the time, and yet he confronted the bullies nonetheless. The gestured felt small, but it gave Ulán the courage to fight back. The boys answered violence with violence and emerged victorious, having injured their attackers enough to cause them both to be suspended from school for some time. Ulán made plans to meet up with the other student again during their time off and he learned that the other boy's name was Masaru.

Ulán and Masaru ended up becoming quite the troublemakers around their neighbourhood. Emboldened by the experience of finally winning a fight, Ulán began to scheme ways that he would finally beat down his uncle and Masaru was all too willing to go along with his plans. It was only as Ulán's suspension was about to end that he learned that Masaru wasn't even a student at the school -- he was a homeless orphan who simply happened to be near the grounds on the day they met. By then, the two of them were essentially best friends.

Some years later, Ulán's sister left Onekama in order to attend a college elsewhere. She was sent away by their mother who wished for Ulán to go with her. However, Ulán's hatred of his uncle had such that he decided to stay behind in order to see the man ruined. Ulán and Masaru had gotten acquainted with the local mob -- individuals to whom Ulán knew his uncle owed money -- and they had plotted for several months to "trap" him for them to take care of.

On a day when his sister had come to visit, Ulán had her take their mother back with her to the city using money he had stolen from their uncle -- money meant for an important payment to the loan sharks who were coming to collect that same evening. When the time came, Ulán and Masaru were sitting in the livingroom, playing a game of dice while Ulán's uncle frantically searched the house. When the man confronted his nephew, Ulán told him that he and Masaru had spent it all, inciting a fight between them. Now older and far more experienced, Ulán and Masaru brutally beat Ulán's uncle, only stopping when the debt collectors came knocking. The pair promptly left the scene, leaving the man to his fate. Ulán and Masaru left town together that night and have never since looked back.

For a few years, the duo have hopped from island-to-island. It was after they realized how much profit there was to be had in the business of adventuring that Ulán wished for them to join an adventurers guild themselves. They set sail to Ethovalon on Kala Island, home to the closest guild at the time. Once arriving, the two of them landed themselves in trouble with the city's authorities when their carelessness caused property damage in the markets. They were offered the chance to work off their sentence with the local guild, the Global Communications Setup Foundation, and Ulán quickly accepted on the behalf of both of them.

To Ulán's dismay, due to the circumstances surrounding his and Masaru's arrest, the two were to be kept under the watchful eye of the guild and the authorities to ensure they did not commit any further crimes. Despite being somewhat unhappy with the team that he was placed on and their current situation, Ulán has been co-operative as he still holds the goal of becoming a seasoned adventurer within the guild itself.
