Summary
  • Masaru was orphaned at a young age. Though he lived in an orphanage, he was often neglected to a point that he was able to wander off of the premises to explored the town on his own.
  • He was largely listless and unsure of what to do with himself until he met a boy named Ulan one fateful day.
  • Ulan had been getting bullied by other students of his school. Seeing this, Masaru stood up for the boy and encouraged him to defend himself.
  • After emerging victorious against the bullies, Ulan quickly befriended Masaru and the two became close friends.
  • Over the years, Masaru grew into an intimidating figure. The local mob approached him to offer him work as a bodyguard or as a debt collector, although he was easily distracted and so they did not often employ him.
  • As Ulan's uncle owed the mob large sums of money and Ulan detested his uncle, he eventually came up with an idea for him and Masaru to "trap" the man and leave him to his fate.
  • The pair stole money set aside for the debt collectors and beat the man bloody when he attempted to fight them over the matter.
  • They left Onekama shortly after and traveled the surrounding islands before deciding to go to Ethovalon to join the GCSF.
  • In Ethovalon, they were arrested for property damage almost immediately and offered a chance to serve their sentence as part of the guild.
  • Under these circumstances, they were assigned to the team Four Seasons and told to keep their antics to a minimum.
Backstory

Masaru was orphaned at a young age due to a house fire that claimed the lives of both his parents. Though he lived at an orphanage for a time, he was often neglected by those in charge of the establishment and so he would roam the town of Onekama unsupervised. He became somewhat of a thief -- albeit often unintentionally -- as he would take food and trinkets off of carts and stalls he passed by. Among the items he stole were books both written and illustrated that would catch his eye and although most vendors treated him harshly, there were a few who were willing to let him peruse their goods and borrow them.

For the most part, he was listless and often didn't know what to do with himself. At some point, he no longer returned to the orphanage as he realized there was no point in returning to a place where nobody cared that he was missing.

One day while he was reading on the playground of a local school, an student was pushed into him, causing them both to fall to the ground. The student was being bullied by his peers and seemed hesitant to defend himself. Seeing this, Masaru acted on his instincts and stood up for the boy himself. Though Masaru didn't realize at the time, his actions allowed for the boy to fight back against the aggressors. Together, Masaru and the boy fought and won against the bullies; an act that resulted in the boy's suspension from school due to the violent nature of their actions. Later, the boy introduced himself as Ulan and he invited Masaru to spend more time with him.

In the ensuing months, Masaru and Ulan grew extremely close. Ulan was very outspoken once he had been given the means to come out of his shell, and so Masaru was happy to follow his lead wherever he went, having finally found something for himself to do. Though they spent much of their time together in their teenage years, it was only once Ulan opened up to Masaru about his own tumultuous home life that Masaru revealed to Ulan that he was essentially homeless himself. From then on, the boys became nigh inseparable.

As the years went on, Ulan became more and more irritable and hateful towards his abusive uncle. As Masaru would do whatever Ulan asked of him, Masaru agreed right away when Ulan hatched a plan to "trap" his uncle and leave him to the mercy of the loan sharks he owed money to. While Masaru was on his own, he was hired on by the local mob at times to be a bodyguard and even a debt collector himself due to his intimidating appearance. Alone, however, he was not very effective at his job and so the mob did not make a habit of employing him often.

After Ulan had moved his mother and sister out of the town, he and Masaru stole a large sum of money that Ulan's uncle had been saving to give to the debt collectors. On the night that the money was due, Ulan's uncle confronted the pair and was promptly beaten down brutally before being left to his fate.

Masaru and Ulan left Onekama together and never looked back. For a time, the two traveled to surrounding islands and took on odd-jobs to fund their lifestyle. At some point however, Ulan had caught wind of how lucrative adventuring as a career could be and proposed that the two of them travel to the nearest adventurers guild to enlist with them. Thus, the pair traveled to Ethovalon with the intent to join the Global Communications Setup Foundation.

Upon arriving however, Masaru had immediately gotten into trouble by causing a fire in the marketplace whilst arguing with a local (with Ulan not doing much to improve the situation either). The two were promptly arrested and offered the chance to work off their sentence with the GCSF, an opportunity that Ulan jumped to accept for the both of them.

Though Masaru still lacks much ambition of his own, he was happy to follow Ulan in joining the guild and becoming a member of Team Four Seasons.
