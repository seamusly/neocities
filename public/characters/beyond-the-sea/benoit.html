Summary
  • Benoit and his mother lived on the cusp of poverty in the town of Emissaint for most of his life. His father was absent due to being arrested and imprisoned under suspicion of treason against the royal family of Soltilaire.
  • Benoit’s mother worked as a housekeeper, and Benoit himself learned how to perform chores on his own. In his teens, he also became a housekeeper to help with their day-to-day expenses.
  • When he was a young adult, he helped a middle-class family prepare extravagant outfits for a gala. Their clothing caught the eye of a prestigious fashion designer named Clarissa from Soltilaire. She came to Emissaint and wished to employ Benoit as her apprentice.
  • Benoit accepted Clarissa’s offer and he moved to Soltilaire to learn from her and help build up her brand and reputation over the next few years. Eventually, the duo became employed by the royal family itself.
  • Not long after the promotion, Clarissa was arrested for stealing valuables from the family and Benoit was unexpectedly thrust into her position as the head of their brand and the royal clothier.
  • Years later, Benoit had built up his reputation and was living well. He moved his mother to Soltilaire and was ready to move on from working for the royals.
  • He petitioned the royal family to allow him to leave so he could start his own business. His request was only partially granted, leaving him dissatisfied with his life more than ever.
  • Around the same time, the rumours of an impending war between Soltilaire and a neighbouring kingdom were spreading. Using his fortune, Benoit purchased property in Ethovalon on Kala Island, and he fled with his mother before the conflict broke out.
  • In Ethovalon, Benoit was able to start his cafe bar, Canvas Nouveau. The establishment saw Theo and Hawke of the GCSF team Syzygy as regulars. One time, Hawke mentioned he’d seen two individuals that resembled figures from the Soltilaire royal court and Benoit let slip that he may have known them.
  • Niccolo Bernardi and Axel Fulhaus eventually visited Benoit at Canvas Nouveau and Niccolo inadvertently dared Benoit to join the GCSF during their bickering.
  • Benoit impulsively asked Theo to allow him to join their team, thus making him a member of the GCSF.

Backstory

Benoit was born in the town of Emissaint on the Dark Continent. His parents once held a decent position in society and they lived much of their lives together in comfort. However, before Benoit was born, his father was arrested for suspected treason and taken to the city of Soltilaire to be put before a jury. Benoit’s mother claims that his father was arrested on false charges, but the true reasons behind the arrest were unknown. As such, Benoit’s father was absent from his life and he and his mother ended up living constantly on the cusp of poverty.

In his youth, the money that Benoit’s mother made went towards providing him an education. She worked as a housekeeper for hire, which often meant that Benoit was home by himself. Out of necessity, he learned how to care for himself and for the house, performing chores as needed whenever he returned from his day out at school. He learned how to mend clothes, cook, and clean and while his mother wished for him to work towards his own aspirations, he ultimately began to work alongside her in his teenage years to help with the family’s expenses as well.

On one fateful day — after becoming a young adult — as Benoit was aiding a middle-class family with their preparations for an upcoming gala at the capital of the Dark Continent, it was discovered that he had a keen eye for fashion. Though he first set out to simply fix up the children’s clothing, the daughter of the family asked him for advice and he put together a tailored outfit for her. At the time, he was merely paid extra for his service. However, when the family returned from their trip, they brought a new opportunity for him that would change his life.

A seamstress from Soltilaire had taken notice of the family’s attire and wished to meet with the designer to get to know them better. The woman — named Clarissa — was a consultant who worked for the royal family and was searching for an apprentice to work under her. Impressed by Benoit’s skills and his humble living, she opted to hire and train him. Benoit, seeing it as a chance to better his and his mother’s life, accepted the offer and moved to Soltilaire to live with his new mentor.

For a few years, Benoit studied under Clarissa, helping her with her various fashion shows and working directly with the royal family as her brand grew in prestige. Only a few months after they were given the honour of being the “official” clothiers for the family however, Clarissa was stripped of her position and promptly arrested. In secret, she had been stealing from the royal family, using the trusting nature of the young prince to do so. Suddenly, Benoit had been put in charge of Clarissa’s brand and it became his own. Though he was stressed by the burden of responsibility being thrust onto his shoulders, he resolved to do his best to please his benefactors so that he could continue his good fortune.

In the years since Benoit took over the royal family’s fashion needs, he primarily kept his head down and did as he was told to maintain and rebuild the reputation of his — mostly unwanted — brand. He became a star of sorts among the elite in Soltilaire and even received deals and recognition outside of the kingdom. At some point, he was able to move his mother to Soltilaire to live with him as well.

But despite his seemingly perfect life and his awe-inspiring “rags-to-riches” story, Benoit was not satisfied with where he had ended up. Though he had a talent for fashion, his passions ultimately lay in food and hospitality. Being forced into a life working for the family had caused deeply-buried resentfulness to fester and he was growing more and more irritated by his position as the years passed.

Encouraged by his mother to take a step towards pursuing his goals now that he was in a position to, he petitioned the royal family to allow him to retire from their employ. His request was only partially approved. Though he was given ownership of a property to begin building an eatery in, he was still expected to be on-call for them until such a time that he took on an apprentice of his own to take over his mantle. The expectation was that he would begin the process of training a protege while the establishment was being properly set up. Until then, he would still be the royal seamster and somebody else would be in charge of the business. Unfortunately, this also meant that he had little control over the business itself at the time, a situation that frustrated him deeply.

Eventually, there were murmurs of impending conflict. Rumours were circulating the city and especially amidst those who gossiped frequenting the taverns and bars, and Benoit caught wind of the frightening news before it was well-known. Unwilling to risk being caught in the middle of a war — and rather fed up with his position in Soltilaire anyway — Benoit and his mother began preparations to leave the Dark Continent entirely. As fate would have it, just before Soltilaire came under siege, the two were able to board a ship that took them away to safety.

Prior to their arrival, Benoit was able to negotiate purchasing property in Ethovalon on Kala Island. Once they properly settled into the city, he and his mother were not left wanting as his wealth was able to afford them housing and a fresh start for his plans to start a business. Benoit became the proprietor of Canvas Nouveau, a cozy and art-themed cafe that doubles as a nightlife bar when the sun goes down. Though he has employees, waitstaff, cooks, and stand-in bartenders as needed, he is still considered the head barista/bartender and he enjoys every second he gets to spend behind the bar.

Canvas Nouveau saw a decent amount of patronage from the explorers of the Global Communications Setup Foundation. Some of the regulars included the members of the Rescuer team Syzygy, a man named Theo and his partner Hawke. On one evening, while Hawke was drunkenly regaling Benoit with a story of their latest adventure, he seemed to describe an individual Benoit was familiar with from his time in Soltilaire. He was eventually able to surmise that Hawke was speaking of Niccolo Bernardi, the Grand Duke of Soltilaire and Axel Fulhaus, the crown prince; both of whom Benoit used to regularly work with and both of whom he was regularly annoyed by.

Though Benoit thought that the matter would be left at that, Niccolo and Axel later visited Canvas Nouveau to see him after they had heard from Hawke that the bartender seemed familiar with them. At first, Benoit was cordial with the pair out of habit. Once he realized that they no longer held their station and that he was no longer being watched by the members of the court however, Benoit dropped the pretense of politeness and began to bicker with Niccolo about their lives since escaping the war in Soltilaire.

Benoit stated that he had never been better, while Niccolo attempted to downplay him by stating that he and Axel were having a fantastic time with the GCSF. Though Axel seemed enthusiastic enough, Benoit argued with Niccolo about how truthful he was being. Ultimately, Niccolo inadvertently dared Benoit to join the GCSF to see if Benoit himself could possibly have a better time than he was at the moment. Agitated and unwilling to let himself be beaten by Niccolo, Benoit approached Theo — who was watching from afar at the time — and asked to join his team.

Not long after that — though he was slightly regretful of his decision — Benoit was given papers to officially make him a part of the GCSF as a member of the team Syzygy.
