Summary
  • Polly was born on the Sand Continent. When they were only a few years old, their parents took them on a short tour around the islands of the Parting Sea.
  • As the family was departing the Snow Continent to return home at the end of their trip, the ship they were on capsized.
  • Polly was saved from drowning by an older Wyrdeer man named Olwen who later adopted them into his family when he discovered their parents did not survive the accident.
  • Though Polly was happy and extremely close with their new family, they struggled to make friends for most of their life.
  • When one of their older siblings left Caister to pursue their own goals, they were encouraged to one day work up the nerve to do the same.
  • One day, a GCSF team called Four Seasons came to town and Polly met one of the team's leaders, a Pumpkaboo named Kessy who was hopelessly lost.
  • After spending the day helping Kessy find her way around, she and Polly agreed to become penpals after she left the island.

Backstory

Apollo -- better known as "Polly" by his friends and family -- was born on the Sand Continent, in a quaint little town near the north coast. His family worked and lived in the town's saloon, an establishment owned by his grandparents that would eventually be passed down to his mother and her siblings. While Polly's mother was accustomed to staying in one place for most of her life, his father had been a more worldly individual who traveled across the Great Ocean in his youth. Only once he decided to start a family with Polly's mother did he settle down.

A few years after Polly was born, he and his parents embarked on what was meant to be a short tour of the Great Ocean to bring some excitement to their lives. The trip took the better part of the year and the family visited the Mist, Water, Air, Grass, and Snow Continents in their travels. When it finally came time to depart from Caister to return to the Sand Continent however, tragedy struck. A critical flaw in the ship's hull caused it to become ruptured mid-journey. In the midst of the chaos and panic of a sudden evacuation, Polly was knocked overboard and subsequently lost consciousness as he was unable to swim.

Polly was pulled out of the ocean and resuscitated by a fellow passenger before he could drown. Much of the rescue efforts that followed the capsizing was a blur to the young Numel. The man who saved them -- an older Wyrdeer -- held onto them until they could receive proper medical attention back on shore in Caister. While Polly was confined to the hospital, the Wyrdeer tried to find their parents, only to discover that neither were among the survivors. In the days that followed, the man continued to visit Polly whilst they were recovering. He introduced himself to them as Olly (which was short for Olwen), and when it came time for Polly to leave the hospital, Olwen took it upon himself to adopt them into his family living in Caister.

In the early days of their new life with the family, Polly was extremely shy and hardly spoke to anybody. It took a few weeks before anybody was able to make a breakthrough with Polly which allowed them to finally be comfortable with their new surroundings. Over the years, they bonded with their family, but still struggled to make many long-lasting friendships in the community. Even so, they were able to grow up in a wonderful, loving family.

As their siblings began to grow into adults, they began to leave Caister to seek new horizons and opportunities. The departures upset Polly, but their siblings tried to encourage him to try to venture out into the world one day as well in the hopes of conquering his fear of the ocean and to experience new things. Though he had no intention of doing so at the time, he took their words to heart and considered it for some time.

On one fateful occasion, a team of the renowned Global Communications Setup Foundation arrived to restock in the town. One of the team's leaders -- a Pumpkaboo -- had gotten separated from the rest of her co-workers and was struggling to read the map of the streets. After seeing her pass by the corner of the street their house was on a few times, Polly approached her to see if she needed help navigating. Grateful for the offer, she accepted and introduced herself as Kessy of the team Four Seasons. After spending a few hours guiding Kessy through Caister and eventually reuniting her with her teammates, Kessy suggested that Polly should stay in touch with her as a penpal and they agreed.

Over the next few months, Polly received letters from Kessy detailing her adventures with the GCSF. He enjoyed reading them and looked forward to whenever she would write to him again. After a few times writing back-and-forth, Kessy formally invited Polly to visit her on Ethovalon to potentially join her team. The idea had Polly conflicted at first. They wanted to see their friend again, but they were terrified of traveling over water again after not doing so since the tragedy. With the encouragement of their family however, they eventually worked up the nerve to do so.

By the time they arrived in Ethovalon and met up with Kessy and her team again, Kessy's own co-leader had stepped down from his position to focus his own familial matters. Because of this, rather than invite Polly to be a recruit on her team, Kessy asked if they would lead Four Seasons with her instead as a team of Wayfarers. Though they had reservations, Olwen suggested that it would be a good way to help Polly get out of their shell and overcome their fear of water if they were to travel the world in the company of friends. Wanting to make both their family and themself proud while helping out a friend in need, Polly accepted the offer and was brought into the fold as one of the new leaders of Four Seasons.
