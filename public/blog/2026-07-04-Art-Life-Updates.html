oops! all wrist pain

About few weeks ago, I injured my wrist in a really silly way. I'm not 100% sure what happened but my best guess is that I had rolled over onto my side in my sleep. When I tried to pull my right arm out from underneath myself in a half-awake state, I hadn't taken the weight off of my arm yet and I pulled my wrist.

Whatever happened, the pain was sharp. I was hoping it'd subside over the next few days but after a week, there were no improvements. I've talked to my doctor about it for the last few weeks as we've been monitoring the situation. While we don't think it's anything terribly serious, it is a very slow-healing injury. It's been about 3-4 weeks now, the pain hasn't gotten much better, and my doctor estimates it'll take another 4-6 weeks before it fully heals.

TL;DR: I injured my wrist and it hurts real bad when I try and turn my hand like this, which I have to do to draw. It could be another 4-6 weeks before it heals.

I do know that there are ways for me to get around this (moving my arm to draw instead of my hand, etc), but I've been drawing like this basically my entire life and it's not an easy adjustment to make. No matter what changes I try to make now to accommodate this, it's going to slow me down.

This is going to affect my drawing output, something I was already struggling with over the last few months. What this does not affect however, is my ability to code. I can still code without too much issue despite this setback. I don't need to turn my wrist like this to type, meaning I can wear my wrist brace when I'm working on code.

So, that leads to my next point...

the waitlist situation

Those on my waitlist have been waiting upwards of 6 months now for me to get to them, and I unfortunately cannot commit to getting to the remainder of my list at this point. I can't in good conscience make people wait any longer for me to get my ass into gear when it's clearly has not happened and will not be happening any time soon, no matter how much I say it will.

The wrist pain is only one part of it. I've also been creatively burnt out for the better part of a year, and continuing to try and push my self to work through it to be able to make some money. I can't do this to myself anymore; especially when I had severely under-priced my art the last time I opened commissions. Lessons have definitely been learned...

I will be refunding my current order once the funds are available in my PayPal account to do so, and cancelling the rest of my waitlisted orders. I've gotten in touch with those on the list about this already.

I would like to take a break from taking art commissions for a while. Ideally, I would take this time to:

  1. let my wrist heal
  2. work on rebuilding my art process and improving my overall art skills

I've been meaning to do this for a long time, but now that I have no choice but to change the way I draw to accommodate my predicament, I think this is as good a time as any.

So, TL;DR:

  • My art commissions are officially on hiatus. My code commissions will remain OPEN! If I haven't opened them back up on VGen yet, expect that soon!
  • I may still accept commissions from friends / returning clients. Everything I've said mostly applies to my public / VGen commissions. Feel free to message if you have an inquiry, but I may decline it.
  • I may respond to specific "looking for" posts about customs or commissions if they really interest me.
  • I likely will still occasionally post adopts both to Toyhouse and / or VGen.
  • If an emergency comes up or if I come up with something fairly simple that I feel comfortable selling (like really small or quick chibis or a YCH), I may reopen art commissions sooner but only for smaller offerings like that.
